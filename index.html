Keanu Reeves

An actor, director and producer

About Keanu Reeves

  • Keanu Charles Reeves was born on september 2, 1964 in Beirut Lebanon

  • He lived in Sydney and New York before settling in Toronto, Canada at the age of six where Reeves spent the most of his formative years.

  • The actor struggled in school, even getting expelled from a performing arts school.

  • Reeves was reportedly about to start a career as a professional ice hockey player, but changed his mind after an injury and a trip to New York

  • After high school, the star took acting courses at night, then joined community theater productions and got an agent. His first professional roles was on a local Toronto television program called "Hanging In," where he played a "tough street kid."

  • But Reeves' big break arguably came in 1989, with "Bill & Ted's Excellent Adventure." Fresh off a role in period drama "Dangerous Liaisons," Reeves' turn as stoner genius Ted garnered him instant acclaim. Although "Bill & Ted" received mixed reviews, Reeves was generally praised for his performance, and the movie was a hit at the box office.

  • Reeves joined the alt-rock band Dogstar as a bassist in the early 1990s, reportedly after an encounter with drummer Robert Mailhouse in a grocery store.

  • In 1994, the understated actor became a big-budget action star with the release of Speed (1994). After all this, Reeves did the unthinkable and passed on the Speed sequel, but he struck box-office gold again a few years later with the Wachowski siblings' cyberadventure, The Matrix (1999).

  • The 1999 sci-fi action film "The Matrix" is arguably one of Reeves' most iconic movies. Directed by the Wachowskis, the film tells the story of a computer hacker enlisted to take down dystopian overlords. While subsequent spin-off films weren't as successful, "The Matrix" franchise made Reeves a household name.

  • Reeves had said in a previous interview that he wanted to settle down and start a family at some point in his life, and in 1999, Syme became pregnant. Tragically, the couple's daughter, Ava Archer Syme-Reeves, was stillborn in December of that year. Syme reportedly experienced postpartum depression, and many believe that the grief the couple experienced due to the loss of their child led them to break up. However, they still remained friends up until Syme's death in April 2001. Reeves was due to begin shooting back-to-back "Matrix" sequels that spring, but took some time to grieve before resuming working.

  • In 2011, Reeves started Arch motorcycle company with motorcycle designer Gard Hollinger, according to a recent GQ profile of Reeves. The company currently has a shop in Hawthorne, California, where Reeves can work on new designs.

  • Keanu produced the 2012 documentary "Side by Side," which focused on the differences and history of both digital and photochemical film techniques.

  • In 2011, Reeves wrote "Ode to Happiness," an illustrated book that the Huffington Post called "a whimsical, humorous poetic riff on self-pity."

  • The 2014 action film "John Wick" was a surprising hit for Reeves both critically and commercially, and would go on to spawn two more sequels.

  • Huffington Post reports that Reeves "quietly" started a private cancer foundation in recent years, as a result of his sister Kim's 10-year battle with leukemia.

  • The actor also reportedly gave much of his earnings from the "Matrix" franchise to the special effects and design teams, and even gave stuntmen working on the films their own Harley Davidson motorcycles, according to Hello Magazine.

  • Some of Keanu Reeves upcoming titles are: John Wick 4, Matrix 4, DC League of Super Pets.
